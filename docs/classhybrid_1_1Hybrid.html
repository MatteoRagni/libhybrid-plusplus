Detailed Description

template<typename REAL_T, std::size_t dim_x, std::size_t dim_u, std::size_t dim_p, std::size_t dim_y, typename... Args>
class hybrid::Hybrid< REAL_T, dim_x, dim_u, dim_p, dim_y, Args >

Abstract class for an hybrid system.

The hybrid system implemented follows:

\begin{align} \dot{t}(\tau) = & 1 & \text{for } (x, u) \in C \\ \dot{k}(\tau) = & 0 & \\ \dot{x}(\tau) = & f(t, k, x, u, p) & \\ \end{align}

\begin{align} t^+(\tau) = & t & \text{for } (x, u) \in D \\ k^+(\tau) = & k + 1 & \\ x^+(\tau) = & g(t, k, x, u, p) & \\ \end{align}

\begin{align} y = & h(x, u, p) \end{align}

where:

  • \( f \) is the flow map;
  • \( g \) is the jump map;
  • \( h \) is the output map;
  • \( C \) is the flow set;
  • \( D \) is the jump set.
  • \( p \) are the function parameters.
  • \( \tau \) is an engine time for the integration of \( t \) and \( k \).

There are some purely virtual functions that must be implemented by the user:

  • the flow map \( f \)
  • the jump map \( g \)
  • the jump set \( D \)
  • the output map \( h \)
  • (as for now, will be removed un future releases) the next method that calls the integrator
  • (iff using an implicit integrator) the Jacobian of the flow map \( Jf \)

The flow map is discretized with a numerical integrator, from which the model shall inherit. For the evolution of the system, both \( t \) and \( k \) are limited by horizons.
